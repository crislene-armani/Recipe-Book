Moqueca Capixaba / Fish Stew

Back to Homepage

Moqueca Capixaba is a traditional Brazilian fish stew from the state of Espírito Santo. This light and flavorful dish features fresh fish, tomatoes, onions, bell peppers, and fragrant herbs, all gently simmered in a clay pot. Unlike other moquecas, it is made without coconut milk or palm oil, highlighting its unique and delicate flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds fresh fish fillets (such as sea bass, grouper, or snapper)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bunch cilantro, chopped
  • 1 bunch parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon annatto (urucum) oil
  • 1/2 cup water or fish broth

Instructions

  1. Season the fish fillets with lime juice, salt, and pepper. Let marinate for 20-30 minutes in the refrigerator.
  2. In a clay pot or heavy-bottomed pan, heat the olive oil and annatto oil over medium heat.
  3. Layer the sliced onions, tomatoes, and bell peppers in the pot. Sprinkle with minced garlic, chopped cilantro, and parsley.
  4. Place the marinated fish fillets on top of the vegetable layers. Pour the water or fish broth around the sides of the pot.
  5. Cover the pot and let the stew simmer gently over low heat for 20-25 minutes, or until the fish is fully cooked and the vegetables are tender.
  6. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot with white rice and a side of pirão (a traditional Brazilian fish sauce made with cassava flour).
