Feijoada

Back to Homepage

Feijoada is a classic Brazilian dish made with black beans, a variety of meats (such as pork, sausage, and beef), and flavorful seasonings. It's a rich, hearty stew typically served with rice, collard greens, and orange slices. Feijoada is often enjoyed during festive occasions, family gatherings, and special celebrations.

Watch the Preparation Video

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried black beans, rinsed
  • 1/2 pound pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 pound sausage (such as chorizo or Brazilian linguiça), sliced
  • 1/2 pound smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1/2 pound bacon, diced
  • 1/2 pound beef short ribs (optional)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 6 cups water or chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional, for extra flavor)
  • 1 bunch collard greens, chopped (for serving)
  • Orange slices (for serving)
  • Rice (for serving)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, soak the black beans in water overnight. Drain and rinse the beans before cooking.
  2. In a separate large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the diced bacon, pork shoulder, sausage slices, smoked sausage, and beef short ribs (if using). Cook until the meats are browned, about 10-12 minutes.
  4. Stir in the soaked black beans, bay leaves, smoked paprika (if using), and water or chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 2 to 3 hours, or until the beans are tender and the flavors are well combined.
  5. Once the beans are cooked, season the feijoada with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the consistency by adding more water or broth if needed.
  6. While the feijoada simmers, sauté the collard greens in a bit of olive oil until tender, about 5 minutes.
  7. Serve the feijoada hot with rice, collard greens, and orange slices on the side. The orange slices help cut through the richness of the dish and add a refreshing contrast.
