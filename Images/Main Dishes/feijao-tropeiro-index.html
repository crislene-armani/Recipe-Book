Feijão Tropeiro / Tropeiro Beans

Back to Homepage

Feijão Tropeiro is a traditional Brazilian dish made with beans, cassava flour (farinha de mandioca), sausage, bacon, and a variety of seasonings. It is a hearty and flavorful dish often served as a side or a main course, perfect for family meals or celebrations.

Watch the Preparation Video

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked black beans (or any type of beans you prefer)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 pound bacon, diced
  • 1/2 pound sausage (such as chorizo or Portuguese sausage), sliced
  • 1/2 cup cassava flour (farinha de mandioca)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional, for extra flavor)
  • 1 boiled egg, chopped (optional, for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil for frying the cassava flour

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the diced bacon and sausage slices to the skillet. Cook until the bacon is crispy and the sausage is browned, about 7-10 minutes.
  3. In a separate pan, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat and fry the cassava flour (farinha de mandioca) until it turns golden brown, stirring occasionally. Set aside.
  4. Once the bacon and sausage are cooked, add the cooked beans to the skillet and stir well. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to combine.
  5. Add the fried cassava flour to the bean mixture and stir to incorporate. Adjust the seasoning with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika (if using).
  6. Stir in the chopped parsley and green onions, and cook for another 2 minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with chopped boiled egg (optional). Feijão Tropeiro is often enjoyed with rice and a side of salad or fresh vegetables.
