Frango Ensopado / Chicken Stew

Back to Homepage

Frango Ensopado is a comforting Brazilian dish made with tender chicken pieces braised in a flavorful tomato-based sauce. This hearty stew is perfect for family meals and pairs beautifully with rice, mashed potatoes, or crusty bread.

Watch the Preparation Video

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 pounds chicken (thighs, drumsticks, or a mix), skin-on and bone-in
  • 1 medium bell pepper, sliced (optional)
  • 4 medium tomatoes, diced (or 1 can diced tomatoes)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and cook until softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
  2. Season the chicken pieces with salt and pepper. Add them to the pot and brown on all sides, about 8-10 minutes.
  3. Add the sliced bell pepper (if using), diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, and chicken broth to the pot. Stir well to combine.
  4. Add the cubed potatoes, sliced carrots, and bay leaf. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover. Let the stew simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  5. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to taste. If the sauce is too thick, add a bit more chicken broth or water.
  6. Serve the Frango Ensopado hot, garnished with fresh parsley or cilantro. Enjoy with rice, mashed potatoes, or crusty bread!
