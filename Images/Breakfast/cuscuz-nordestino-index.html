Cuscuz Nordestino / Northeastern Couscous

Back to Homepage

Cuscuz Nordestino is a traditional Brazilian dish made with cornmeal, a staple food in the northeastern region of Brazil. This savory dish is often enjoyed for breakfast or lunch, served with a variety of toppings or fillings like cheese, eggs, or meat.

Watch the Preparation Video

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cornmeal (fine or medium grind)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Optional: grated coconut or cheese for topping
  • Optional: eggs or cooked vegetables for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cornmeal and salt. Gradually add the water, mixing until the cornmeal absorbs the liquid.
  2. Let the mixture sit for about 10 minutes to allow the cornmeal to soften.
  3. Place a pot with a steamer basket over medium heat, adding water to the bottom (but not touching the steamer). Bring to a boil.
  4. Transfer the cornmeal mixture to the steamer basket and cover. Steam for about 15â€“20 minutes, stirring occasionally to ensure even cooking.
  5. Once cooked, remove from the heat and stir in butter until melted and smooth.
  6. Optional: Add grated coconut or cheese into the cooked cuscuz, or serve with eggs or vegetables on top for extra flavor.
  7. Serve hot, and enjoy your Cuscuz Nordestino as a breakfast or side dish.
