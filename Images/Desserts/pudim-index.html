Pudim / Flan

Back to Homepage

Pudim is a beloved Brazilian dessert, similar to flan or crème caramel. This smooth and creamy custard is topped with a golden caramel sauce, making it a decadent treat for any occasion. Pudim is easy to prepare and a crowd favorite!

Watch the Preparation Video

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugar (for caramel)
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (14 ounces) whole milk (use the same can as the condensed milk for measurement)
  • 3 large eggs

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a small saucepan over medium heat, melt the sugar until it turns into a golden caramel. Pour the caramel into a bundt pan or round baking dish, tilting it to coat the bottom evenly. Set aside to cool.
  3. In a blender, combine the sweetened condensed milk, whole milk, and eggs. Blend until smooth.
  4. Pour the custard mixture over the cooled caramel in the baking dish.
  5. Place the baking dish in a larger roasting pan. Fill the roasting pan with hot water until it reaches halfway up the sides of the baking dish, creating a water bath (bain-marie).
  6. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the pudim is set and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  7. Remove from the oven and let the pudim cool to room temperature. Then refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  8. To serve, run a knife around the edges of the pudim to loosen it. Invert it onto a serving plate, letting the caramel drizzle over the top. Enjoy!
