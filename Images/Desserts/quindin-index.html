Quindim / Egg Custard

Back to Homepage

Quindim is a traditional Brazilian dessert made with egg yolks, sugar, and coconut. This rich, glossy, and golden custard is baked to perfection, creating a creamy center and a lightly toasted coconut crust. Quindim is a simple yet indulgent treat that’s perfect for any occasion.

Watch the Preparation Video

Ingredients

  • 10 large egg yolks
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup shredded coconut
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • Butter and sugar (for greasing the molds)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease individual molds or a round baking dish with butter and coat with sugar. Set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk the egg yolks until smooth. Strain the yolks through a fine sieve to remove any membranes.
  3. Add the sugar, shredded coconut, melted butter, and coconut milk to the yolks. Mix until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into the prepared molds or baking dish, filling them about 3/4 of the way.
  5. Place the molds in a large roasting pan. Fill the roasting pan with hot water until it reaches halfway up the sides of the molds, creating a water bath (bain-marie).
  6. Bake for 30-40 minutes (or longer for a large dish), or until the quindim is set and the top is golden.
  7. Remove from the oven and let cool to room temperature. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.
  8. To serve, run a knife around the edges to loosen the quindim. Invert onto a serving plate, revealing the glossy golden top. Enjoy!
