Canjiquinha / Cornmeal Soup

Back to Homepage

Canjiquinha is a traditional Brazilian dish made with crushed corn kernels (canjica), pork, and a savory broth. This hearty and flavorful soup is perfect for cozy meals, often served during colder months or festive occasions.

Watch the Preparation Video

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 lb pork ribs or pork belly, cut into small pieces
  • 1 cup crushed corn kernels (canjica or hominy corn)
  • 6 cups chicken or pork broth
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup green peas (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional, for extra flavor)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, cooking until softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the pork ribs or pork belly pieces to the pot and cook until browned on all sides, about 7-10 minutes.
  3. Pour in the chicken or pork broth, bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for 20â€“25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Add the crushed corn kernels (canjica), diced carrots, and green peas (if using). Continue simmering for another 30-40 minutes, or until the corn is tender and the soup has thickened.
  5. Season the soup with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika (if desired). Stir well and adjust the seasoning to taste.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley or cilantro. Canjiquinha is often served with crusty bread or rice on the side for a hearty meal!
